## 13th–15th Century Conflicts
### Crusades

#### First Crusade (1096–1099)
- **Goal**: Reclaim Jerusalem from Muslim rule
- **Key Events**: Siege of Antioch, capture of Jerusalem
- **Result**: Crusader victory; establishment of Christian states in the Levant

#### Second Crusade (1147–1149)
- **Goal**: Recapture Edessa (lost to Muslims)
- **Result**: Failed; loss for Crusaders

#### Third Crusade (1189–1192)
- **Key Figures**: Richard the Lionheart (England), Saladin (Ayyubid)
- **Result**: Truce with Saladin; Christians allowed limited access to Jerusalem

#### Fourth Crusade (1202–1204)
- **Goal**: Conquer Muslim lands but diverted to Constantinople
- **Result**: Sack of Constantinople (1204), weakening Byzantine Empire

#### Later Crusades (5th–9th, 13th century)
- **Less effective**, more politically driven
- Included the **Children’s Crusade**, **Albigensian Crusade**, **Crusades in the Baltic** (Northern Crusades)

**Impact**:
- Intensified Christian-Muslim tensions
- Boosted Mediterranean trade and contact with Islamic world
- Weakened Byzantine Empire
- Spread of Crusading ideology into Europe

### Mongol Conquests (1206–1368)
- **Regions Affected**: Eurasia
- **Key Points**:
  - Established the largest contiguous empire in history.
  - Devastated regions across Asia and Europe.
  - Notable invasions: China, Persia, Russia, Eastern Europe.

### Hundred Years' War (1337–1453)
- **Combatants**: England vs. France
- **Key Points**:
  - Series of conflicts over the French throne.
  - Introduction of longbow and decline of chivalric warfare.
  - Significant battles: Crécy, Poitiers, Agincourt.

### Wars of Scottish Independence (1296–1357)
- **Combatants**: Scotland vs. England
- **Key Points**:
  - Struggle for Scottish sovereignty.
  - Figures like William Wallace and Robert the Bruce emerged.
  - Culminated in the Treaty of Edinburgh-Northampton.

## 16th–17th Century Conflicts

### Ottoman–Habsburg Wars (1526–1791)
- **Regions Affected**: Central and Eastern Europe
- **Key Points**:
  - Series of conflicts between the Ottoman Empire and Habsburg Monarchy.
  - Battles for control over Hungary and the Balkans.

### Thirty Years' War (1618–1648)
- **Combatants**: Various European powers
- **Key Points**:
  - Religious and political conflict within the Holy Roman Empire.
  - Devastated large parts of Europe.
  - Ended with the Peace of Westphalia.

### Anglo-Spanish War (1585–1604)
- **Combatants**: England vs. Spain
- **Key Points**:
  - Marked by naval battles and privateering.
  - Included the defeat of the Spanish Armada in 1588.

## 18th Century Conflicts

### War of the Spanish Succession (1701–1714)
- **Combatants**: European coalitions
- **Key Points**:
  - Triggered by the death of the childless Charles II of Spain.
  - Prevented the unification of French and Spanish thrones.

### Seven Years' War (1756–1763)
- **Combatants**: Major European powers
- **Key Points**:
  - Often considered the first "world war."
  - Fought in Europe, North America, and Asia.
  - Set the stage for British dominance in India and Canada.

### American Revolutionary War (1775–1783)
- **Combatants**: American colonies vs. Great Britain
- **Key Points**:
  - Led to the independence of the United States.
  - Involved France, Spain, and the Netherlands as allies to the colonies.

---

## 19th Century Conflicts

### Napoleonic Wars (1803–1815)
- **Combatants**: Napoleonic France vs. various European coalitions
- **Key Points**:
  - Spread revolutionary ideals across Europe.
  - Ended with Napoleon's defeat at Waterloo.

### Crimean War (1853–1856)
- **Combatants**: Russia vs. Ottoman Empire, Britain, France
- **Key Points**:
  - Conflict over territories and religious rights in the Holy Land.
  - Notable for the role of Florence Nightingale and modern nursing.

### American Civil War (1861–1865)
- **Combatants**: Union vs. Confederacy
- **Key Points**:
  - Centered on issues of slavery and states' rights.
  - Resulted in the abolition of slavery in the U.S.

---

## 20th Century Conflicts (Pre-WWI)

### Russo-Japanese War (1904–1905)
- **Combatants**: Russia vs. Japan
- **Key Points**:
  - First major victory of an Asian power over a European one.
  - Signaled the rise of Japan as a world power.

### Balkan Wars (1912–1913)
- **Combatants**: Balkan League vs. Ottoman Empire; then among Balkan states
- **Key Points**:
  - Aimed to seize territories from the weakening Ottoman Empire.
  - Set the stage for tensions leading to WWI.

## 21st Century Conflicts

### War on Terror (2001–present)
- **Regions Affected**: Global
- **Key Points**:
  - Initiated after the September 11 attacks.
  - Includes wars in Afghanistan and Iraq.

### Syrian Civil War (2011–present)
- **Combatants**: Syrian government vs. various rebel groups
- **Key Points**:
  - Complex conflict involving international powers.
  - Humanitarian crisis with millions of refugees.

### Russian Invasion of Ukraine (2022–present)
- **Combatants**: Russia vs. Ukraine
- **Key Points**:
  - Ongoing conflict with significant global implications.
  - Involves issues of territorial integrity and international law.
